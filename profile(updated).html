Personal Recommendation For You

For individuals with obesity, high-impact exercises can place excessive strain on joints, especially the knees and ankles, increasing the risk of injury. Therefore, we recommend light, effective exercises that help burn calories, improve fitness, and support weight loss.

Exercise
Swimming

Swimming is a low-impact exercise that engages almost all muscle groups. The water reduces pressure on the joints, making it safe and effective for burning calories.

Walking

Walking is a simple, light exercise that can be adjusted in intensity. Start with short durations (10-15 minutes) and gradually increase over time.

Stationary Cycling

Stationary cycling provides excellent cardiovascular benefits with minimal joint strain. The intensity can be adjusted to suit individual capabilities.

Lifestyle
Proper Sleep

Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep per night. Lack of sleep can increase ghrelin (hunger hormone) and decrease leptin (satiety hormone). Avoid caffeine or heavy meals at least 2 hours before bedtime. Maintain a consistent sleep schedule, even on weekends.

Food Recommendations

Oatmeal: High in fiber, helping you stay full longer.

Salmon: A great source of protein and omega-3 fatty acids for heart health.

Leafy Greens: Such as spinach, broccoli, and kale, which are low in calories but nutrient-dense.

Low-Sugar Fruits: Such as apples, strawberries, and oranges.

Almonds: A healthy snack rich in good fats.

Summary

By following these exercise and lifestyle recommendations, you can gradually lose weight, reduce health risks, and improve your overall quality of life. Always consult a doctor or nutritionist before making significant changes to your routine.
